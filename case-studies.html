Rún Bifröst

Multi-state HR for a fully-remote workforce, rebuilt from the inside.

Situation

A fully-remote workforce had expanded into more than thirty states with a stack of HR tools built for a smaller, simpler company. Multi-state leave accruals were being reconciled by hand each pay period. Compliance posting obligations were tracked across two spreadsheets and a shared calendar. The HR function had grown into a one-person bottleneck holding the firm right with the law in jurisdictions it had never legally tested.

Intervention

  • Structured audit of HR workflows across all operating states, surfacing 14 distinct exposure points ranked by severity
  • Phased implementation of a custom HR information system — payroll-adjacent, multi-state aware — replacing the manual reconciliation entirely
  • Migration of leave accrual logic into rule-based modules per state, with automated posting-obligation tracking
  • Reporting suite built to replace seven manual spreadsheets with five reconciled, refreshable reports

Architecture choices worth noting

Multi-state leave logic was modeled as state-scoped rule sets rather than hard-coded conditionals — making future state additions a configuration change, not a code change. Compliance-relevant fields were tagged at the schema layer so that audit trails are generated automatically.

Outcome
Reconciliation time reduced from a full week per cycle to under an hour. Compliance exposure surface reduced by 14 of 14 identified findings, with audit-trail evidence available on demand.

Draupnir

The onboarding workflow that finally finished itself.

Situation

A growing professional-services firm onboarded new hires through a workflow held together by a checklist in a shared document, a calendar reminder, and the institutional memory of one operations lead. Roughly one in six onboardings missed a required step — an I-9 verification, a benefits enrollment window, a system access request. The fix every time was reactive: someone noticed, someone scrambled, someone apologized.

Intervention

  • Single automation built end-to-end: trigger on new-hire record, branching logic per role, tracked across systems
  • Integration with the firm's existing document signing and benefits enrollment tools — no replacement, no migration
  • Failure notification path with a defined escalation rule so a stalled step surfaces within 24 hours
  • Written operating documentation handed off at close, plus a 30-day warranty window
Outcome
Onboarding completion rate moved from ~83% on first pass to 100% across the first 18 hires following deployment. Operations lead recovered an estimated 4–6 hours per hire.
"The most useful thing about working with Michael is that the system we ended up with was unmistakably ours — built around how our team actually operates, not how a generic tool wanted us to operate." — Operations Lead, anonymized client

Mímir

A benefits Q&A that cites its sources.

Situation

A mid-sized employer with a complex benefits plan was answering the same questions every open-enrollment cycle. HR fielded an estimated two hundred individual questions over six weeks — most of them already answered in the summary plan description, the FAQ, or the benefits guide. The HR team had the documents. Employees had the questions. The bridge between them was a Slack thread that nobody had time to maintain.

Intervention

  • Scoped Mímir-class assistant built over the existing benefits corpus — SPD, FAQ, guide, three carrier documents
  • Every answer cited the specific document and section it came from, with a link to the source
  • Confidence thresholds calibrated to escalate to human HR when the model's grounded answer fell below tolerance
  • 30-day evaluation period with an accuracy review against an agreed test set before sign-off

What we deliberately did not do

The assistant was not given any access to individual employee records, salary data, or PII. The corpus was static documentation only. Personal-situation questions ("what does this mean for me?") were routed to human HR by design.

Outcome
Open-enrollment Q&A volume to HR dropped by roughly 65% in the first cycle following deployment. HR's remaining workload shifted to the harder, judgment-required questions — which was the point.

Rún

An audit that stopped a hire before it became a problem.

Situation

An early-stage company was preparing to hire its first employees in California, New York, and Washington — three states whose employment law surface area is dramatically larger than the founder's home state. Leadership was not sure what they didn't know, and the existing PEO relationship had not flagged the obligations the upcoming hires would trigger. They asked for a Rún audit before the offer letters went out.

Intervention

  • Structured review of the proposed hire roster against state-by-state employment requirements
  • Mapping of policy gaps — handbook addenda, notice obligations, sick leave accruals, final-pay rules
  • Written findings report with severity rankings and a 30/60/90-day remediation sequence
  • Walkthrough session with leadership and the firm's outside counsel before any hire was finalized
Outcome
Eleven distinct compliance gaps identified before first hire. Remediation completed in five weeks; first offers extended with the firm's exposure documented and bounded.
"Worth every dollar. We thought we needed a tool. What we actually needed was someone to tell us what we didn't know — and to put it in writing." — CEO, anonymized client
